HISTORY

Arunodaya Trust, a grassroots NGO in Andhra Pradesh, was founded by development activists and social workers with a background in rural development. This gave the organization two key advantages: starting with experienced field professionals and adopting a broad socio-economic and environmental development perspective. Since 2008, Arunodaya Trust has focused on rural wage issues, women’s rights, and child rights through a rights-based approach, which continues to guide its programs today.

ORGANIZATIONAL BELIEFS

Arunodaya Trust is dedicated to addressing poverty through a holistic approach, tackling interconnected issues for comprehensive development.

    1. Core Issues:
  • Arunodaya Trust focuses on key issues such as wage movement, land rights, livelihoods, food security, health, women’s rights, empowerment, fundamentalism, and market exploitation.
    • 2. Holistic Approach:
  • Poverty is seen as a result of interconnected problems that need to be addressed simultaneously for sustainable development.
    • 3. Sectoral Diversity:
  • Unlike many NGOs, Arunodaya Trust works across all sectors of development, rather than specializing in just one or two areas.
    • 4. Community Participation:
  • True development occurs when the community identifies its needs and takes the lead in the change process.
    • 5. Transparency:
  • Arunodaya Trust maintains a firm stance against corruption and bribery, earning respect from the community and setting an example for other organizations

ACCOUNTABILITY

Arunodaya Trust's accountability is based on five pillars: Christian commitments, serving children and communities, shaping industry standards, ongoing learning, and transparency. Current focus areas include:

  • Feedback from children and communities on services.
  • Transparency in reporting and financial disclosure.
  • Protection from Sexual Exploitation and Abuse (PSEA).
  • Anti-corruption efforts.

These pillars guide the Trust's mission to promote child well-being and uphold integrity.
